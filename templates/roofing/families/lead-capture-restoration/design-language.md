# Lead Capture Restoration Design Language

Niche: roofing

## Reference Summary

Designed for quick cold outreach mockups when the lead has strong contact info but limited brand assets. It should be practical, conversion-forward, and local: roof restoration, repairs, gutters, free inspection, phone-first contact.

## Visual Thesis

Local roofing lead capture: immediately says what they do, why to call, and how to request a free inspection, with enough visual substance to avoid looking like a text flyer.

## Typography Direction

- Display: confident sans-serif or sturdy serif, not delicate.
- Body: plain readable sans-serif.
- Labels: strong chips for services and trust points.

## Color System

- Dominant: warm white or light neutral.
- Accent: strong roof red, orange, or trade green depending on source material.
- Support: dark charcoal for footer and contrast bands.
- CTA: phone/contact button must stand out.

## Imagery

- hero: roofer working / roof restoration / exterior home with roof visible.
- proof: before-after placeholder structure without fake customer claims.
- contact: form next to roof image or phone-first CTA.
- service: icons or small photos for restoration, repairs, gutters, pressure cleaning.

## Layout Patterns

- hero: phone-first, service area, free inspection CTA.
- services: clear bullet/service grid from provided lead copy.
- why us: common-sense benefits, clearly marked as positioning not verified claims.
- process: inspect, quote, restore, cleanup.
- contact: simple form and big phone number.
- footer: compact with contact links.

## Best Fit

- Low-info lead from photo/OCR/flyer/signage.
- No website found, but phone and services are clear.
- Fast one-page demo to test interest.

## Do

- Show phone and contact path above the fold.
- Use provided services exactly when available.
- Use dummy copy only to make modules complete, not to fake proof.

## Do Not

- Build a huge multi-page site from very little info.
- Invent email, address, reviews, project count, or exact service area.
- Use internal audit language in customer-facing copy.
