What's Working Well

Before we talk about improvements, let's recognise what's already doing its job:

  • Your phone number stands out on desktop. Visitors on a computer can find your number straight away — that's half the battle.
  • Your quote button is easy to spot. The coral-coloured button draws the eye and tells people exactly what to do next.
  • Your services are clearly listed. On mobile, the service menu makes it easy for customers to scan what you offer without hunting around.

What's Holding You Back

We've spotted a handful of issues that are quietly costing you customers. The good news? They're all fixable.

1. Browsers are warning customers away

Your website doesn't have HTTPS security — that means every visitor sees a "Not Secure" warning in their browser. Some browsers even show a full-screen alert before letting people through.

What this costs you: Google has ranked sites without HTTPS lower since 2018, and over 80% of visitors close the tab the moment they see that warning. For a business relying on Google to bring in customers, that's a lot of lost calls.

2. Mobile visitors can't find your number

On a phone, your header only shows a small phone icon — the actual number 1300 734 148 isn't visible.

What this costs you: Most local searches happen on mobile. When someone's ready to call, they want to see the number immediately. If they have to work for it, they'll hit the back button and call the next roofer on the list.

3. You're not showing up properly in Google search

Your website is missing the technical details Google uses to display businesses in search results — things like your address, opening hours, and services in a format Google can read.

What this costs you: Without this, you miss out on the map listings, knowledge panels, and featured snippets that competitors are getting. And when people use ChatGPT or Google's AI to find a roofer, you're invisible.

4. The design feels a bit dated

Your homepage photo is washed out, and the angled overlay across the middle makes the site feel like it hasn't been updated in a while.

What this costs you: Customers decide whether to trust a business in the first few seconds. If the website looks old, they assume the business might be too — and they go looking for someone who feels more current.

5. There's no trust proof at the top

The first screen shows your logo, menu, and buttons — but nothing that tells a new visitor "you can trust us." No reviews, no licence details, no proof you've done this work before.

What this costs you: Roofing is expensive, and people are cautious. When they can't immediately see proof you're legitimate, they comparison-shop instead of calling.

The pattern we're seeing: Your website has good bones, but small friction points are quietly turning away customers who would otherwise call. Most of these fixes take hours, not weeks.

What Changes When We Fix This

Here's what you can expect once these issues are resolved:

  • More calls from mobile. A visible tap-to-call number and clearer layout means customers don't have to think — they just ring.
  • Better Google visibility. Proper markup means you'll start appearing in map packs, AI summaries, and knowledge panels where your competitors currently dominate.
  • Higher trust at first glance. A sharp hero image, your 4.6-star rating up front, and clear licence details tell visitors they're in the right place.
  • Fewer people bouncing. Right now your mobile performance score is 41 out of 100 — slow sites lose impatient customers before the page even finishes loading. Optimising images and cleaning up unnecessary scripts will fix that.
  • More quote requests. Your contact form currently asks for eight pieces of information. Research shows every extra field cuts conversions — we'd trim that to three or four.
