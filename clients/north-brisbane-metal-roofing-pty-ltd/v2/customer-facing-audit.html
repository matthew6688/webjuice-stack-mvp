What's Holding You Back

These are the specific issues that are likely costing you business right now. Each one translates directly to fewer phone calls or lost customers who chose a competitor instead.

1. Your site shows "Not Secure" warnings
Your website doesn't have HTTPS security enabled. This means browsers display a "Not Secure" warning in the address bar, and some browsers even show a full-screen warning that blocks your page. More than 80% of visitors will close the tab when they see this. Google also penalises sites without HTTPS in search rankings, which means fewer people find you in the first place. For a business with zero Google reviews, this warning is driving away visitors before they even read about your services.
2. Mobile visitors can't easily call you
On mobile phones, your header shows a green phone icon but doesn't display your actual phone number. Visitors have to guess whether the button works or hunt for the number elsewhere. Most people searching for roofing services on mobile want to call immediately and compare quotes from several businesses. If your number isn't obvious, they'll simply go back to Google and try the next company. Even losing 10–20% of potential calls adds up quickly.
3. No trust signals in the first five seconds
When someone lands on your homepage, they see a beautiful photo and a quote button, but no immediate proof that you're licenced, insured, or trusted by other customers. Roofing is a high-value service, and customers make snap judgements about trust. Without visible reviews, licence details, or local proof points in the first screen, some visitors won't even scroll down or submit a quote request.
4. The headline may confuse homeowners
Your main headline says "Trusted by Queensland's Premium Builders" and focuses on new builds for builders and developers. If a local homeowner who needs roof repairs or replacement sees this, they might assume you don't take their type of job. Unclear messaging means people leave even when they're a perfect fit for your services.
5. Google can't understand your site properly
Your website is missing the technical markers that help Google (and newer AI search tools like ChatGPT) understand that you're a local Brisbane roofing business. There's no structured business information, your page title doesn't include your business name and service type, and the heading structure confuses search engines. This means lower rankings, missed opportunities to appear in Google's local map results, and complete invisibility when someone asks an AI assistant "Who's a trusted roofer in North Brisbane?"

What Changes When We Fix This

Here's what you can expect when these issues are addressed:

  • More phone calls. A visible tap-to-call number on mobile and removal of the "Not Secure" warning will stop visitors from bouncing. You'll convert more of the traffic you're already getting.
  • Higher Google rankings. Proper HTTPS, correct page structure, and business information that Google can read will improve your position in local search results. Better rankings mean more people find you before they find your competitors.
  • More quote requests from the right customers. Clear messaging that speaks to both homeowners and commercial clients, combined with visible trust signals (licence, insurance, reviews), will increase the number of qualified enquiries you receive.
  • Visibility in the new AI search era. ChatGPT and similar tools now handle billions of searches each month. When someone asks "Who should I hire for metal roofing in Brisbane?", properly structured website information means you'll actually be recommended.
  • Faster load times and better mobile experience. Optimised images and a properly designed mobile layout make your site feel more professional and trustworthy, which directly impacts whether someone picks up the phone.
