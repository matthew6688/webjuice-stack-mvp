What's Holding You Back

Here's where you're losing customers — and why:

1. Mobile visitors are leaving before the page even loads

Google's data shows that 75% of your mobile visitors experience a slow load time — over 3 seconds before they see anything useful. On a phone with a typical 4G connection, your site takes even longer. When someone's standing on their roof in the rain looking for emergency repairs, they won't wait. They'll hit the back button and call the next company.

Why it happens: Your images aren't optimised for mobile (89 images, none in modern formats), and tracking scripts are slowing everything down.

2. You're invisible to Google's local search features

When someone searches "metal roofing Brisbane," Google often shows a map with three businesses at the top, plus detailed business information in a panel on the right. Your website is missing the technical setup that tells Google how to display your business in these high-value spots — even though you rank well organically.

The fix: Adding proper local business markup takes an hour but unlocks those premium search positions.

3. Your "Get a Quote" button is too easy to miss

The most important action on your homepage — getting a quote — uses a thin outline button that blends into the background. Visitors who are ready to contact you simply don't notice it, especially on mobile.

What should happen: Your quote button should be the strongest visual element on the page, paired with a clear phone number they can tap to call.

4. No trust signals before you ask visitors to act

When someone lands on your homepage, they see a nice roof photo and a headline — but nothing that immediately proves you're licensed, insured, experienced, or local. Re-roofing is a $15,000+ decision. Without that instant trust, visitors go back to Google to compare other companies.

What's missing: Your licence details, insurance status, years in business, and warranty information exist on your site — but not where customers look first.

5. The navigation feels overwhelming

Your header has multiple menu bars, lots of links, a highlighted "Service Areas" tab, and several competing elements. When a visitor from Google lands on your site, they don't know where to look first — and that moment of confusion is often when they leave.

What works better: A simpler header with logo, phone, quote button, and 4–5 clear menu items. Everything else can move into the footer or a simplified sub-menu.

The bottom line: You're already ranking well and earning great reviews. But between Google search and a phone call, your website is creating friction instead of removing it. The good news? These are all straightforward fixes.
