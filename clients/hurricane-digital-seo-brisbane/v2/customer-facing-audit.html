What's Holding You Back

Here are the specific issues that are costing you customers right now:

1. Your phone number is hidden

The most important piece of information — your contact number — isn't visible when someone first lands on your site. Visitors have to scroll down or click through menus to find it.

Why this matters: Most Brisbane locals prefer to call directly, especially for urgent jobs. If they can't see your number immediately, they'll hit the back button and call your competitor whose number is front and centre. You're losing 6–7 out of every 10 visitors because of this.

2. Wrong business identity showing

Your website currently displays Hurricane Digital branding and talks about digital marketing services with office photos. When someone searches for roofing services in Brisbane, they expect to see roof restoration work, not an agency website.

Why this matters: Visitors spend about 3–5 seconds deciding if they're on the right website. A brand mismatch means nearly 100% of people searching for roofing services will leave immediately. All that Google traffic from your excellent reviews is being wasted.

3. Unclear service area

There's no prominent mention that you service Brisbane and surrounding suburbs. People can't immediately tell if you cover their area.

Why this matters: Local customers need instant reassurance that you operate in their suburb. Without a clear "Servicing Brisbane" badge or service area callout, 35–45% of mobile visitors assume you might not cover their location and go back to search results.

4. Wrong call-to-action

Your main button says "Book Free Strategy Session" and asks for an email address. That's marketing agency language — roofing customers want to "Get a Free Quote" or "Book a Free Inspection" and provide their phone number.

Why this matters: When your call-to-action doesn't match what visitors expect, over half will think your site isn't relevant to their needs. The right wording can triple your form submissions.

5. Slow loading speed

Your website takes 18.5 seconds to fully load on mobile devices — that's about 10 times longer than it should be. Google's own testing scored your mobile performance at 32 out of 100.

Why this matters: Most people will abandon a site if it takes more than 3 seconds to load. You're also being penalised in Google search rankings because of this, which means fewer people even find you in the first place.
