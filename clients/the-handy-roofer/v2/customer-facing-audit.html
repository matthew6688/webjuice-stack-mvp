Your website has some good foundations, but a few key issues are costing you enquiries every week.

What's Working Well

Let's start with what you're doing right. These are strengths worth keeping:

Your phone number is easy to find

Your phone number shows up clearly on both desktop and mobile without visitors needing to scroll. That's important – most local customers prefer to call rather than fill out a form.

Your roofing photos show the right work

The roof photography on your site is relevant and immediately shows the type of work you do. Visitors can see you're a real roofing business right away.

You're actively updating your website

Your site was last updated just 14 days ago, and you have 13 blog posts. That tells Google you're an active business, which helps with search rankings.

You're showing experience and warranty

Mentioning your 20+ years of experience and workmanship warranty helps build trust with potential customers.

What's Holding You Back

Now let's look at the specific issues that are likely costing you enquiries. These are fixable, and the impact of fixing them can be significant.

Mobile visitors can't easily request a quote

On mobile phones, only the heading "Request a Free Quote" is visible when someone first lands on your site. The actual form fields are hidden below – visitors have to scroll down to find them.

Impact: Most local searches happen on mobile. If someone can't immediately call you or start a quote, they'll often just go back to Google and click on the next roofer.
Your quote form asks for too much information upfront

The quote form on the right side of your desktop site asks for name, email, phone, job address, description, and file upload all at once – before someone has even decided if they want to work with you.

Impact: Local customers often just want to ask about pricing or confirm you service their area. Long forms discourage people from filling them out, especially mobile visitors coming from Google.
The first screen feels crowded and overwhelming

Your homepage hero area uses two large orange text boxes over the roof photo, with oversized text taking up much of the screen. It's a lot to take in at first glance.

Impact: Customers make a snap judgement in the first few seconds about whether a company looks professional. If the first screen feels cluttered or hard to read, many will click back to Google and try the next business.
Trust signals aren't visible enough

While you show your Google rating, visitors can't see your licence details, insurance information, or project examples in the first screen. For a big decision like roof replacement, people need reassurance.

Impact: Roofing is a major expense. When comparing three to five companies at once, customers will choose the one that makes them feel most confident – and visible proof of licensing, insurance, and past work makes a real difference.
Google can't tell what your pages are about

Your pages are missing the heading tags that help Google understand what each page is focused on. Without these signals, Google has a harder time showing your site for the right searches.

Impact: Two identical pages, one with proper headings and one without, can rank five or ten positions apart. That's the difference between page one and page three in search results.
Your site loads slowly on mobile

Google's own testing tool gives your site a performance score of 54 out of 100 on desktop. About 90% of your site's download size comes from tracking tools rather than your actual content. Many of your images are still in older formats that take longer to load.

Impact: Slow sites lose visitors. Google's research shows that when load time goes from one second to three seconds, the chance of someone leaving jumps by 32%.

What Changes When We Fix This

Here's what you can expect when these issues are addressed:

More mobile enquiries: A simplified mobile experience with a prominent call button and shorter quote form means fewer people will bounce back to Google. More visitors will become leads.
Better first impressions: A cleaner, less crowded homepage with visible trust signals (licence, insurance, warranty, project photos) will make visitors feel more confident reaching out.
Higher Google rankings: Proper heading structure, faster load times, and optimised images will help Google understand and rank your pages better for local roofing searches.
Stronger trust and credibility: Displaying your credentials clearly – ABN, insurance, certifications, before-and-after photos – separates you from competitors who don't show this information.
Future-proofed for AI search: As more people use ChatGPT and Google's AI features to find local services, having the right technical structure means you'll appear in AI-generated recommendations, not just traditional search results.
