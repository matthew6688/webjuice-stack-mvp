What's holding you back

Right now, your website has a few problems that are directly costing you customers. Here's what we found:

Desktop visitors see an error page instead of your business

When someone opens your website on a laptop or computer, they see a "403 - Forbidden" error message instead of your roofing company. Many Brisbane homeowners research on their computer first, comparing a few companies side-by-side. When they hit an error, they go straight back to Google and call your competitor instead.

People can't find how to contact you in the first five seconds

Research shows that 70% of mobile visitors decide whether to stay or leave within 8 seconds. If they don't immediately see a clear "Get a quote" or "Call us" button, most will close the tab. You've earned 147 great reviews, but if visitors can't quickly work out how to reach you, that trust doesn't turn into calls.

Your phone number isn't tap-to-call on mobile

On a smartphone, your phone number appears as plain text. Customers have to copy it, switch to their phone app, paste it, then dial. Most won't bother—they'll just tap the back button and call the next roofer whose number works with one tap.

No licence or insurance details visible

Before spending thousands on a roof job, Queensland homeowners want to see your QBCC licence, insurance coverage, and how long you've been in business. When that information isn't front and centre, people either ring to ask (extra friction) or move on to a competitor who displays it clearly.

The site looks dated

Roofing is a big investment, and customers pay close attention to whether a business looks professional and current. When a website feels a few years behind, people worry the quality of work might be behind too—and they choose someone whose site feels more modern and trustworthy.

Google can't understand your site properly

Your website is missing the behind-the-scenes code that helps Google show you in map results, knowledge panels, and AI-generated answers. Even if you rank well for searches, you're invisible in the high-conversion spots where customers make quick decisions. With ChatGPT and Google AI now answering "who's a good roofer in Brisbane?", sites without this structure simply don't get mentioned.

  • Better visibility in search and AI. Adding the right technical structure means Google can feature you in map packs, knowledge panels, and AI overviews—the places where customers make decisions without clicking through to five different websites.
  • A site that reflects the quality of your work. A modern, professional design tells customers that you care about details and run a tight operation. It reinforces the trust your reviews have already built.
