Your website has solid foundations, but a few key changes could bring you significantly more enquiries.

What's Working Well

Your phone number is easy to find
People looking for a roofer want to call straight away, and your number is clearly visible at the top of the page. That's exactly where it should be.
You've built trust with real customers
Your 5-star rating from 17 Google reviews is excellent social proof. People trust what other Brisbane residents say about your work.
You're showing the right credentials
Your site mentions you're licensed and have public liability insurance – essential trust signals in the roofing industry.

What's Holding You Back

People on phones don't see why they should trust you
When someone opens your site on their mobile, the first thing they see is your logo and menu – but no photos of your work, no customer reviews, and no proof you're the right choice. Most people searching for roofers in Brisbane are on their phone, and they'll usually decide within 5 seconds whether to keep reading or go back and try someone else. Right now, you're losing some of those visitors before they even scroll down.
Your quote form asks for too much information
Your contact form has 8 fields. Industry research shows that every extra field you ask someone to fill in reduces the number of people who'll complete it. Most roofing sites that get good enquiry rates ask for just 3-4 things: name, phone, suburb, and what's wrong with the roof. The simpler you make it, the more enquiries you'll receive.
The site looks older than it should
Roof restoration isn't a small purchase – people are trusting you with one of their home's most important systems. When your website looks dated (the large banner image, the floating share buttons on the side, the "Go to..." mobile menu), it can make potential customers wonder whether your service is equally behind the times. A fresh, modern look signals that you're a professional, current business.
The site loads slowly on phones
Your website takes almost 9 seconds to fully load on a typical mobile connection. Most people give up after 3 seconds. The main culprits are images that haven't been optimised for the web – they're unnecessarily large files that take too long to download. Every second of delay costs you potential customers.
You haven't been able to see who visits your site for two years
Your website is still running Google's old tracking system (Universal Analytics), which stopped collecting data in July 2023. That means you've had no visibility into how many people visit your site, where they come from, which pages they look at, or whether they're calling or filling in the quote form. You're flying blind when you could be making decisions based on real data.

What Changes When We Fix This

More mobile visitors turn into phone calls and enquiries
When people on phones immediately see real photos of Brisbane roofs you've restored, your 5-star rating, and a simple way to get in touch, they're far more likely to contact you instead of hitting the back button.
Your website feels as professional as your work
A clean, modern design with sharp photos of your completed projects gives people confidence that you're a current, active business that takes pride in quality.
The site loads fast enough that people don't leave
Properly optimised images and a streamlined site mean your pages load in 2-3 seconds instead of 9, so you stop losing impatient visitors.
You can see what's actually working
With modern analytics properly set up, you'll know how many people visit each week, which services they're interested in, and whether they're finding you through Google search, your Google Business Profile, or somewhere else. That lets you double down on what's working.
You show up when people ask AI for recommendations
More and more people are asking ChatGPT or Google's AI "Who's a good roofer in Brisbane?" Instead of showing a list of 10 websites, AI gives a short answer and only mentions businesses whose websites have the right structure. Right now, your site isn't optimised for this – but it's a straightforward fix that future-proofs your visibility.
