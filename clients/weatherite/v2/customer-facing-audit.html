What's holding you back

Over 70% of people searching for a roofer in Brisbane are doing it on their phone. Right now, your website is losing most of those people within the first 8 seconds. Here's why:

1. Mobile visitors can't see how to contact you

When someone opens your site on a phone, the entire screen is filled with your headline. The contact button isn't visible — they have to scroll down to find it. Research shows mobile users leave within 8 seconds if they can't immediately see how to take action.

What this costs you: Most of your Google traffic right now is from mobile. If 7 out of 10 people are leaving before they even see your contact button, you're losing the majority of potential customers who found you through search.

2. Your phone number is hidden

Right now, visitors need to click a button to see your actual phone number. When someone is comparing three or four roofing companies, they'll call the one where the number is visible immediately — and skip the ones where it's hidden.

What this costs you: You're losing the comparison shoppers. These are ready-to-book customers who just want to get three quotes quickly — and they're choosing your competitors because it's easier.

3. No reviews visible on your homepage

88% of people check reviews before contacting a local tradesperson. Your Google rating is 5 stars from 4 reviews — but none of that shows on your website. A visitor landing on your homepage has no immediate proof that you're trustworthy.

What this costs you: In a high-value service like roofing (often thousands of dollars), people need to see social proof immediately. Competitors who display star ratings above the fold are winning the trust race before you even get a chance.

4. You're only targeting The Redlands

Your main headline says "Specialists in The Redlands" — nothing about Brisbane. Someone in Bayside or South-East Brisbane sees that and assumes you don't service their area, so they leave and find someone else.

What this costs you: The broader Brisbane metro area has far more search volume than The Redlands alone. Every day you're missing potential customers from surrounding suburbs who would absolutely use your services — they just think you're too far away.

5. The design looks dated

Your website's visual style — the heavy red colour overlay, rounded gradient buttons, and flat navigation — matches templates that were popular around 2016-2017. That might not seem important, but research shows people form a first impression in 0.05 seconds.

What this costs you: A website that looks ten years old makes visitors wonder "Is this business still operating?" or "Are they keeping up with modern standards?" Even if your actual work is excellent and your prices are competitive, an outdated website makes people choose a competitor who looks more current.

What happens when we fix this

More calls from Google. When mobile visitors can immediately see your contact button and phone number without scrolling, conversion rates typically increase by 40-60% for local service businesses.

You win the comparison game. Displaying your phone number and star rating prominently means ready-to-book customers choose you first when they're comparing options.

Professional first impression. A modern, clean design with natural photography (not heavy colour overlays) signals that you're an active, current business that takes pride in how you present yourself.

Faster loading on phones. Your current site scores 56/100 on Google's mobile speed test. Optimising your images and cleaning up unnecessary code typically cuts load time in half — which directly impacts whether people stay or leave.

Other opportunities

Beyond the immediate conversion issues, there are a few foundational gaps worth addressing:

  • Trust signals: Your site shows your ABN and mentions free quotes, but it's missing insurance details, warranty information, years in business, and trade certifications. These are standard trust markers that local customers look for — especially for high-value work like roofing.
  • AI search visibility: Tools like ChatGPT and Google's AI search are becoming a major source of local business research. Your site is missing the structured data that these tools rely on, which means you're invisible when someone asks "Who are the best roofers in Brisbane?"
  • No social presence: There are no links to Facebook, Instagram, or any other platform where people can see more of your work or follow up after visiting your site. This means if someone isn't ready to call today, you've lost the chance to stay on their radar.
