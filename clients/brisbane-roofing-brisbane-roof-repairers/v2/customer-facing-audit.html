What's Holding You Back

Your homepage looks unfinished
First impressions matter — and right now, visitors aren't sure if you're a real business.

The main headline on your site says "YOUR COMPANY FOR ROOF REPAIRS BRISBANE" instead of your actual business name. It looks like placeholder text that was never replaced. When someone lands on your site from Google, they make a snap judgement in about 3 seconds — and if it looks like a template, many will click back and call your competitor instead.

Mobile visitors can't easily call you
More than half your visitors are on phones — and they're struggling to find your number.

On mobile, there's only a small phone icon at the top with no visible number next to it. People have to guess that tapping it will call you. Most roofing enquiries happen on mobile (someone spots a leak and Googles "roof repairs near me" on their phone). If they can't see your number clearly, they'll call the next company on the list.

Nothing tells visitors why they should trust you
Roof repairs are expensive and risky — customers need proof you're legitimate before they call.

When someone first lands on your homepage, they see buttons asking them to contact you, but no proof of why they should. Your 5-star Google rating isn't shown. There's no mention of being licensed or insured, how long you've been in business, or what areas you cover. Customers shopping for roof repairs are cautious — they want to see credentials, guarantees, and social proof before picking up the phone.

Your site loads slowly on mobile
Slow loading times mean lost customers — especially on phones with poor reception.

Your mobile site takes over 8 seconds to fully load. Most people will give up after 3 seconds and go back to Google. The main culprit is unoptimised images — you're serving large JPG files that could be 30-50% smaller in modern formats. Every extra second of loading time costs you enquiries.

You're invisible to AI search
More people are asking ChatGPT and Google's AI for local recommendations — and your site isn't showing up.

When someone asks an AI assistant "Who's a good roofing company in Brisbane?", the AI can only recommend businesses whose websites have the right technical setup. Your site scores 30 out of 100 on AI discoverability — which means you're missing out on a fast-growing search channel. This includes missing structured data for your services, ratings, and business credentials.
