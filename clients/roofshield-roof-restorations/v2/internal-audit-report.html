设计观感的整体诊断

The website uses a standard, functional layout with a generic stock photo and a heavy visual overlay that obscures the service being offered, making it feel like a template rather than a specialized local business.

CRITICAL hero_image_overlay
Hero image obscured by dark overlay

观察到The main hero image (a house with a roof) is covered by a heavy, dark blue/black gradient overlay that makes the photo look dim and indistinct.

为什么是问题Customers searching for roof restoration want to see *results*. By darkening the image, the site hides the quality of the work. It feels like a generic template rather than a showcase of real work. It creates a 'mood' rather than demonstrating competence.

正确长啥样A bright, high-contrast photo of a finished roof restoration with no dark overlay, or a very subtle gradient only at the bottom to ensure text readability.

redesign 怎么改Remove the dark overlay. Use a high-quality, bright photo of a completed roof restoration. Ensure the text is legible by using a drop shadow or a semi-transparent white text box, rather than darkening the whole image.

MAJOR generic_stock_photo
Generic stock photo instead of real work

观察到The hero image appears to be a generic stock photo of a suburban house, not a specific project completed by Roofshield in Brisbane.

为什么是问题Local customers want to see work done in *their* area. A stock photo feels impersonal and untrustworthy. It doesn't prove the business has the capability to handle local roof types (e.g., specific tile or metal common in Brisbane).

正确长啥样A real photo of a roof restoration project completed by the business, ideally with recognizable Brisbane architecture or landmarks, or a photo of the team at work.

redesign 怎么改Replace the stock photo with a high-resolution, real photo of a recent roof restoration project in Brisbane. Add a caption or badge saying 'Restored in [Suburb Name]' to build local relevance.

MAJOR cluttered_navigation
Cluttered navigation with too many links

观察到The top navigation bar has 6 main links plus a dropdown, plus a secondary row of 4 links below it. This creates a 'menu overload'.

为什么是问题A customer searching 'roofing Brisbane' has a specific intent: get a quote or call. They don't want to browse 'Case studies' or 'Gallery' immediately. Too many choices cause decision paralysis. The 'Get Your Free Quote' button is also buried in the nav, not prominent enough.

正确长啥样A simplified navigation with 3-4 key links (Services, About, Contact) and a prominent, high-contrast 'Get a Quote' or 'Call Now' button in the top right.

redesign 怎么改Reduce the top navigation to 3-4 essential links. Move 'Case studies' and 'Gallery' to the footer or a secondary menu. Make the 'Get Your Free Quote' button a distinct, high-contrast element (e.g., red or orange) in the top right corner, separate from the nav links.

证据截图 · Element: header, nav

MAJOR weak_cta_hierarchy
Call-to-action buttons lack visual hierarchy

观察到The two buttons in the hero section ('Call Us Today' and 'Get Your Free Quote') are identical in style (white outline, same size).

为什么是问题When two buttons look the same, the user doesn't know which one is the primary action. This dilutes the conversion focus. One action (usually 'Get Quote') should be visually dominant to guide the user.

正确长啥样One primary button (e.g., 'Get Your Free Quote') filled with a bold color (red/orange) and one secondary button (e.g., 'Call Us') as an outline or text link.

redesign 怎么改Make 'Get Your Free Quote' a solid, high-contrast button (matching the brand red). Make 'Call Us Today' a secondary outline button or a simple text link with a phone icon. This creates a clear visual path for the user.
