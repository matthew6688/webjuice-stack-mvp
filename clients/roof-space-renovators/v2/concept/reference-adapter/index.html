FAQ

Honest answers to the questions homeowners ask.

If yours isn't here, call us — we'll tell you straight whether your roof space is suitable and what it will cost.

Do I need council approval for skylights or attic access?

Most skylight installs and attic ladders are exempt development in Queensland and don't need council approval. Full roof space conversions with structural changes or habitable rooms typically do. We'll tell you which applies during the site inspection and can help with the paperwork if needed.

How much does a skylight installation cost in Brisbane?

A single fixed skylight including flashing and installation typically costs $1,800–$3,200 depending on size, brand, and roof type. Opening roof windows with remote control or rain sensors are $2,800–$5,500. We give a fixed written price after the site inspection — never an estimate over the phone.

Can you install skylights in tile and metal roofs?

Yes. We work with all roof types — terracotta tile, concrete tile, Colorbond steel, and timber shingle. Each requires different flashing and weatherproofing, which is why we inspect the roof before quoting.

Will skylights leak or let in water during storms?

Not if installed correctly. We use manufacturer-approved flashing kits, flexible sealants, and proper overlapping with existing roof layers. All our skylight installs come with a 10-year workmanship warranty covering leaks and water entry.

How long does a roof space conversion take?

A basic attic ladder and flooring install usually takes 1–2 days. Full conversions with skylights, insulation, electrical, and stairs typically take 3–5 days for standard homes. We'll give you a precise timeline in the written quote.
