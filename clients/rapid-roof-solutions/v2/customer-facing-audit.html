Your website does some things well, but it's losing you phone calls and enquiries every week. The good news? Most of what's holding you back can be fixed quickly.

What's Working Well

Your message is clear

Visitors immediately understand you do roof installations and repairs. The headline works, and your quote buttons are visible on both desktop and mobile.

Mobile layout keeps the call button handy

The sticky quote button at the bottom of the mobile screen makes it easy for people to get in touch while they're scrolling.

You're displaying your licence and ABN

Showing your credentials is important in the roofing industry, and you've got the basics covered.

What's Holding You Back

Your phone number is hidden on mobile

Most Brisbane locals search for roof repairs on their phone. When they land on your site, they see a "Call Now" button but not your actual number. Many people prefer to see the number before tapping — and if they don't, they'll hit the back button and call your competitor instead. This is costing you calls every single day.

No Google reviews yet

You've got a 5-star rating, but zero reviews showing. Google's local search ranking depends heavily on review count — not just the star rating. Right now, every roofing company with even a handful of reviews will rank higher than you. The fix is simple: ask every happy customer to leave a quick review when the job's done.

Google can't see what you do

Your website's title tag (the bit that shows up in Google search results) doesn't mention roofing or Brisbane. And the behind-the-scenes code that tells Google you're a local business is missing. Result? You're nearly invisible in local searches, and you won't show up in those handy map listings or knowledge panels on the right side of Google.

No trust signals above the fold

When someone lands on your homepage, they don't see your Google rating, how long you've been in business, or proof that you're insured. Roof work is expensive and risky — people look for these trust markers in the first 8 seconds. Without them, fewer visitors will bother requesting a quote.

The hero image feels too distant

Your main homepage image is a dark aerial shot of a large commercial roof. It's hard to see the quality of your work, and it doesn't feel like the kind of home most Brisbane residents live in. A brighter, closer shot of a finished residential roof would connect better with your core customers.
