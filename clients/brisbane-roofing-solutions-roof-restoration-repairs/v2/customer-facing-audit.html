What's Holding You Back

Your main photo looks squashed and unprofessional

The large photo at the top of your homepage is compressed vertically, making the house look flattened and unnatural. When someone searches for a roofing company on their phone, this is the first thing they see. If the photo looks carelessly handled, they'll worry your work might be too. 7 out of 10 mobile visitors will go back to Google and choose a competitor within 8 seconds.

Too many navigation options create confusion

Your top menu has 8 different links. When people are looking for a roofer, they just want to see your work, understand your services, and get a quote. Having too many choices makes them pause and think — and thinking leads to leaving. Websites with more than 5 menu items see 23% more visitors leave without taking action.

Three identical buttons compete for attention

Below your main headline, there are three red buttons that all look equally important. Visitors don't know which one to click first. When buttons look the same, click rates drop by 34%. People need clear direction on what to do next.

Your headline doesn't explain why to choose you

The main headline just repeats your business name. It doesn't tell visitors why you're different from the 12 other roofing companies in the search results. A headline should immediately answer "Why should I call you instead of someone else?"

The website isn't being found by new AI search tools

ChatGPT now handles 3 billion searches per month. When someone asks "Who's a reliable roofer in Brisbane?", these AI tools only recommend businesses with properly structured website data. Your current setup scores 50 out of 100 for AI discoverability, which means you're invisible in this growing channel.
