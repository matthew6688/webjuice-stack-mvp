设计观感的整体诊断

The site has a functional layout with a clear lead capture form, but the visual presentation feels dated due to the use of drop shadows, generic stock photography, and a lack of authentic business imagery.

MAJOR hero_image_authenticity
Generic stock photo hurts local trust

观察到The hero background image is a generic stock photo of a house with a blue roof, overlaid with a heavy dark gradient. It does not appear to be a photo of the business owner, their team, or a specific job site in Brisbane.

为什么是问题Local service customers are skeptical of 'faceless' companies. A generic image suggests a national franchise or a scam rather than a local expert. It fails to build the personal connection required for high-ticket home services.

正确长啥样A high-quality, authentic photo of the actual business owner or a team member standing in front of a branded van or a completed roof project in a recognizable Brisbane suburb.

redesign 怎么改Replace the stock photo with a professional headshot of the owner or a 'before and after' split image of a real local project. Remove the heavy dark overlay to let the image breathe.

MAJOR form_visual_weight
Form looks like a pop-up overlay

观察到The contact form is placed inside a semi-transparent dark box with a drop shadow, floating on top of the hero image. It looks like a modal or a pop-up rather than part of the page structure.

为什么是问题Users are trained to ignore or close pop-ups. By making the form look like an intrusive overlay, you are subconsciously telling the user to dismiss it. It also creates visual clutter and makes the text harder to read.

正确长啥样A clean, white, solid background form integrated into the layout (e.g., a two-column layout with text on the left and form on the right), or a distinct section below the fold with a clear heading.

redesign 怎么改Remove the semi-transparent background and drop shadow. Use a solid white card with a subtle border or a distinct background color that contrasts with the hero image, ensuring the form feels like a permanent part of the page.

MINOR typography_hierarchy
Weak headline hierarchy and font choice

观察到The main headline 'Brisbane Roof Restoration Specialists' uses a generic sans-serif font with a heavy drop shadow. The sub-headline is also white text on a dark background, making it hard to distinguish from the main title.

为什么是问题The drop shadow on the text gives it a 'Web 2.0' (circa 2010) feel, which makes the business look outdated. The lack of clear hierarchy means the visitor doesn't immediately know what the unique selling point is.

正确长啥样Clean, bold typography without drop shadows. The main headline should be large and clear, with a sub-headline that highlights a specific benefit (e.g., '10-Year Warranty' or 'Free Inspections') in a contrasting color.

redesign 怎么改Remove all text shadows. Use a modern, readable font. Increase the contrast between the headline and sub-headline by using size and weight differences rather than just color.

MINOR cta_button_redundancy
Redundant phone number buttons

观察到There is a red 'Call (07) 3132 1605' button in the top navigation, and another identical red button in the hero section below the text.

为什么是问题While having multiple CTAs is good, having two identical buttons in the same visual area creates visual noise. The user's eye doesn't know where to focus first.

正确长啥样One primary CTA in the hero section (e.g., 'Get a Free Quote') and a clear phone number in the header. The hero button should focus on the form, while the header focuses on immediate calls.

redesign 怎么改Keep the phone number in the header as a text link or small button. In the hero section, use a single, prominent button that says 'Get Your Free Quote' to drive form submissions, or 'Call Now' if calls are the primary goal, but not both in the same spot.
