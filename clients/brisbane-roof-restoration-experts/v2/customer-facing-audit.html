What's Holding You Back

These issues aren't about what you do — your work speaks for itself. They're about what visitors see in the first five seconds, and whether they trust you enough to pick up the phone.

The quote form asks for too much, too soon

Your desktop form asks visitors to fill in nine fields before they can request a quote — name, phone, email, location, roof type, scope of work, age, and a description. That's a lot to ask someone who just found you on Google.

What this costs you: Every extra field you ask for reduces the number of people who finish the form. Roofing customers want a quick way to say "I'm interested" — the detailed questions can come later.

Mobile visitors can't see a way to get a quote

On a phone, the first screen shows your number and headline, but there's no visible button or link to request a quote. Many people browse during work hours or in public places where they can't call immediately.

What this costs you: You're losing every mobile visitor who wants to enquire but can't call right now. A simple "Get Quote" button would capture those leads.

There's no proof of trust visible at the top

Your first screen doesn't show your 4.9-star rating, how long you've been in business, your warranty, or that you're insured. Visitors have to scroll down and hunt for reasons to trust you.

What this costs you: Roofing is a big investment. When people can't immediately see you're established, reviewed, and legitimate, many will click back to Google and call someone else.

The visual design looks dated

The logo, hero image treatment, and heavy text shadows make the site feel older than it is. For a service as important as roof restoration, customers expect a website that looks current and professional.

What this costs you: First impressions matter. A dated website makes some visitors assume your business isn't actively trading or keeping up with modern standards.

You're invisible to AI search

ChatGPT, Google's AI overviews, and other new search tools need structured information to recommend businesses. Your site is missing the technical setup that helps AI understand who you are, what you do, and why you're trustworthy.

What this costs you: When someone asks an AI "which Brisbane roofing company should I call?", you won't be in the answer — even though your reviews and service are excellent.
