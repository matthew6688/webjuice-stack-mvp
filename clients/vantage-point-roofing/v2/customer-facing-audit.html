What's Holding You Back

Mobile visitors can't easily contact you

The problem: When someone visits your site on their phone, there's no clear "Call Now" or "Get a Quote" button visible on the first screen. They see your logo, a menu icon, and a lot of text – but the action button is hidden.

Why it matters: Most people find local roofers on their phone, often while they're comparing multiple businesses. If they can't immediately see how to contact you, they'll hit the back button and call the next roofing company instead. You're losing ready-to-buy customers at the first hurdle.

Your contact form asks for too much information

The problem: Your quote form has 10 fields to fill out, with 7 of them marked as required. That's a lot to ask before someone's even spoken to you.

Why it matters: Industry data shows that every extra form field drops completion rates. Someone who's ready to get a quote right now will often abandon a long form and try a competitor with a simpler one. Three to four fields (name, phone, suburb, brief message) is the sweet spot for roofing quotes.

Missing credentials that Queensland customers expect

The problem: While you're showing your QBCC licence, your website doesn't display your ABN, public liability insurance details, how many years you've been in business, or workers compensation compliance.

Why it matters: These aren't optional nice-to-haves – Queensland homeowners actively look for these before hiring a tradie. When they're comparing you against another roofer who does show all this information, they'll often choose the business that feels more transparent and legitimate, even if your work is just as good.

You haven't been able to see your website data for over two years

The problem: Your site is still running Google's old "Universal Analytics," which stopped collecting data in July 2023. That means for more than two years, you've had no visibility into how many people visit your site, which pages they look at, or where your leads come from.

Why it matters: You're flying blind. If you're spending money on Google Ads, Facebook, or any other marketing, you have no way to know if it's working. You can't see if people are bouncing off your contact form, or which service pages get the most interest. You're leaving money on the table because you can't optimise what you can't measure.

Desktop visitors have to hunt for the quote button

The problem: On desktop computers, your main "Get a Quote" button sits in the far top-right corner of the navigation bar, while your headline and service description are in a large panel on the left. People read your value proposition but then have to search for what to do next.

Why it matters: The easier you make it to take action, the more people will. When someone's convinced and ready to get a quote, every extra second of hunting around the page is a chance for them to get distracted or change their mind. The button should be right where they're already looking.

Your website has a solid foundation – good content coverage, established domain authority, and the right trade credentials. What's missing are the conversion elements that turn visitors into customers, and the visibility to know what's working. Those are fixable problems, not fundamental ones.
