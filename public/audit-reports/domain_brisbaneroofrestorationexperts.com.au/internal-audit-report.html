CRITICAL hero_overlay_dark_unreadable
Dark hero overlay obscures house photo and headline

观察到A dark semi-transparent overlay covers the entire left-side hero image of a house, making the photo nearly black and reducing the headline 'Brisbane Roof Restoration Specialists' to low contrast white text on dark grey.

为什么是问题A local homeowner searching 'roof restoration Brisbane' wants to instantly see work that looks like their own home. When the hero photo is almost entirely blacked out, the site looks broken or unprofessional — the visitor loses confidence before reading a single word.

正确长啥样A bright, high-resolution before/after photo of a restored Brisbane roof (no overlay or a subtle bottom gradient only), with the headline rendered in a bold sans-serif at 40px+ on a clean background area so it reads instantly without straining.

redesign 怎么改Remove the dark overlay entirely. Use a photo with the sky in the upper third and the roof prominent. Place the headline text over a semi-opaque white card on the left side rather than directly on the image, ensuring WCAG AA contrast.

MAJOR form_checkbox_overload
Quote form has 20+ checkboxes visible above fold

观察到The right-side 'Get a FREE Quote Today!' panel contains at least three separate checkbox groups — Services, Scope of Work, Work Type, and Roof Age — all visible in a single vertical scroll. The checkboxes are small (approx 12px targets) and tightly spaced.

为什么是问题A visitor who just wants a quick quote is immediately confronted with a survey. Research shows every additional form field reduces completion. Presenting 20+ micro-decisions before they have even read the business's credentials causes decision fatigue and form abandonment.

正确长啥样A three-field form: Name, Phone, and a single open textarea for 'Describe your roof issue'. A bold 'Get My Free Quote' button. Additional service detail can be collected after contact is made.

redesign 怎么改Reduce the quote form to Name + Phone + optional message. Move checkbox detail collection to a secondary step or the follow-up phone call. Make the CTA button full-width and red/orange to match the nav button colour.

MAJOR logo_illegible_small
Logo text is too small and difficult to read

观察到The top-left logo reads 'BRISBANE roof restoration experts' but the lower text lines are rendered at approximately 8–9px, making the business name effectively illegible without zooming in.

为什么是问题A local visitor who found this site via Google Maps or a search ad needs to immediately confirm they are on the right business's website. An unreadable logo creates uncertainty — 'is this the right company?' — and signals an amateur build.

正确长啥样A two-line logo: 'Brisbane Roof Restoration' at 18–20px bold, 'Experts' or tagline at 13–14px. Minimum logo height 40px with clear whitespace. Alternatively, a simple wordmark at one font size that reads at arm's length.

redesign 怎么改Redesign logo as a single-weight wordmark at minimum 18px for the primary text. Use a roofline or house icon at 32px if icon is desired. Ensure it reads clearly on both white and dark backgrounds.

MAJOR no_trust_signals_above_fold
No reviews, licences, or years-in-business above the fold

观察到The visible above-fold area contains only the dark hero with headline and a quote form. There are no star ratings, review counts, licence badge, insurance logo, 'X years in Brisbane' text, or any third-party credibility markers anywhere in the viewport.

为什么是问题A homeowner hiring a tradie to work on their roof is making a high-stakes decision. They need social proof immediately — a Google review count, a QBCC licence number, or a 'Trusted by 500+ Brisbane homeowners' line — before they'll take any action. Without it, the site could be anyone.

正确长啥样A trust bar directly below the nav or overlaid on the hero containing: Google star rating + review count (e.g. '4.9 ★ 127 Google Reviews'), QBCC licence number, and 'Serving Brisbane for X years' — all visible without scrolling.

redesign 怎么改Add a horizontal trust strip below the nav: Google Reviews badge (pulled via API or screenshot), QBCC licence badge, and a '15+ years experience' stat. Keep it to one row, white background, dark text, 14px.

MINOR cta_button_color_clash
Nav phone button red does not match page CTA hierarchy

观察到The nav bar has a red 'Call (07) 3132 1605' button. The hero section has a separate grey-blue 'Call (07) 3132 1605' button in a different colour and style. The form submit button is not visible in the screenshot.

为什么是问题Two different button styles for the same action (calling the business) confuses the visual hierarchy. A visitor scanning the page can't instantly identify the single most important action. The grey-blue hero CTA looks secondary or disabled compared to the red nav button.

正确长啥样One consistent CTA colour across all phone call buttons — red or orange to convey urgency. The hero CTA button should be the largest version of that same button, not a different colour/style.

redesign 怎么改Standardise all call-to-action buttons to a single brand colour (the existing nav red is fine). Remove the grey-blue variant. Make the hero CTA button at minimum 48px tall and full-width on mobile.

MINOR generic_stock_photo_feel
Hero background photo appears generic rather than showing local work

观察到The hero background shows a residential house with a dark overlay. The house appears to be a generic stock-photo-style suburban home rather than a clearly identified Brisbane property or a before/after restoration shot.

为什么是问题Local service customers respond to proof that the business has worked in their neighbourhood. A generic house photo — especially one so dark it's hard to see — provides no evidence of quality or local presence.

正确长啥样A crisp, bright photograph of an actual completed Brisbane roof restoration job, ideally a terracotta or Colorbond roof with blue sky, with a caption like 'Sunnybank Hills, Brisbane — 2024 restoration'.

redesign 怎么改Source 3–5 real job photos from the client. Use the clearest one as the hero background with no overlay. Add a small caption in the bottom-left corner naming the Brisbane suburb.

需要保留的优点

  • Phone number is prominently displayed in the nav bar as a red call-to-action button — one of the most important conversion elements for a local tradie site.
  • The quote form is positioned above the fold on desktop so a motivated visitor can immediately start an enquiry without scrolling.
  • Navigation is simple and flat (Home / About / Services / Contact) — easy to scan and not overwhelming.

Redesign 优先级

  1. 1. Replace dark hero overlay with a bright real-job photo and add a one-row trust strip (Google reviews + QBCC licence) directly below the nav.
  2. 2. Reduce the quote form to 3 fields (Name, Phone, Message) and standardise the CTA button colour to the existing nav red.
  3. 3. Increase logo legibility to minimum 18px and ensure business name reads clearly on both light and dark backgrounds.
