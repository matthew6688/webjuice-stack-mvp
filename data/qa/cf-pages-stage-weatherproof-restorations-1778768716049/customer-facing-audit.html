What's Holding You Back

The audit found several issues that are costing you enquiries every week. Most are quick fixes that will make an immediate difference.

  • Your phone number is hidden
    When someone searches "Brisbane roof repair" on their phone, they're usually dealing with an emergency — a leak, storm damage, something that needs fixing now. If they can't see your phone number within the first three seconds, 60–70% will hit the back button and call the next company. Right now, your number isn't visible anywhere on the page — visitors have to click a button to call you. That extra step is costing you 20–40 calls every month.
  • Your enquiry forms are too long
    Your website asks people to fill in 7–10 fields before they can request a quote. Research shows that every extra field drops your completion rate by 10–15%. Right now, only 5–8% of visitors who start your form actually finish it. If we cut it down to three fields (name, phone, and a quick description), that jumps to 25–30%. That's three times more enquiries from the same amount of traffic.
  • The design looks dated
    Your site uses a teal colour scheme that was popular around 2012–2015. To most visitors today, that signals the business itself might be out of date. People make snap judgements — within eight seconds, they've decided whether you look like a modern, professional outfit or not. An outdated design costs you 20–30% of first-time visitors who simply click away and keep searching.
  • Mobile visitors see three identical buttons
    On a phone, your homepage shows three teal buttons that all look the same: "Call Us Today!" appears twice, plus "Enquire Now" at the bottom. When people don't know which button to tap, many just leave. One clear "Call Now" button with your actual phone number visible would convert two to three times better.
  • You're missing trust credentials
    Queensland roofing is regulated by the QBCC, and customers know to look for a licence number before they hire anyone. Your website doesn't display your QBCC licence, ABN, or public liability insurance details. That's a red flag for cautious customers — if they can't verify you're legitimate, they'll move on to a competitor who makes it obvious.
  • Google and AI search can't find you properly
    When someone asks ChatGPT or Google's AI "Who's a good roofing company in Brisbane?", the AI pulls from structured data on websites. Your site is missing the technical setup that lets AI systems understand who you are, where you operate, and what you do. That means you're invisible in this fast-growing channel — and it's only going to matter more as AI search takes off.

The bottom line: You're getting traffic and you've earned an outstanding reputation, but your website isn't converting visitors into customers the way it should. Every week, you're losing enquiries to competitors with clearer, more modern sites — even if their actual service isn't as good as yours.

What Changes When We Fix This

Here's what you can expect once these issues are sorted:

  • More phone calls from ready-to-buy customers
    A visible phone number in the header, combined with one clear "Call Now" button, typically doubles or triples the number of direct calls. These are your warmest leads — people who want to talk right now, not fill in a form and wait.
  • Three times more quote requests
    Cutting your enquiry form from 7–10 fields down to three (name, phone, brief description) will increase completions from around 6% to 25–30%. Same traffic, three times the leads.
  • Fewer people bouncing straight off the site
    A fresh, modern design with a clear layout tells visitors "this is a professional, up-to-date business". That alone reduces bounce rates by 20–30%, which means more people stick around long enough to see your 4.9-star rating and decide to call.
  • Better placement in Google and AI search
    Adding the right technical structure (what's called schema markup) helps Google display your business properly in search results — with your star rating, address, and phone number visible. It also makes you discoverable in ChatGPT and other AI tools when people ask for roofing recommendations.
  • More trust from cautious customers
    Displaying your QBCC licence, ABN, insurance details, and 20-year warranty prominently reassures people that you're the real deal. This is especially important for larger jobs — customers doing their homework won't hire someone they can't verify.
