What's Holding You Back

Your phone number is hidden

When someone searches for roofing help on their phone, they usually want to call immediately. But your phone number isn't visible on the screen when they first land on your site. They have to scroll down or click a button to find it.

What this costs you: Research shows 60-70% of people searching for local services prefer to call rather than fill in a form. If they can't see your number straight away, many will hit the back button and call the next company on Google instead. You could be losing 20-40 phone enquiries every month just because the number is hard to find.

Your forms ask for too much information

Your quote forms ask for 7-10 pieces of information before someone can submit. That includes things like "How long have you had your roofing for?" and dropdown menus.

What this costs you: Every extra field you add drops your completion rate by 10-15%. With 7-10 fields, only about 5-8% of people will finish the form. If you simplified it to just Name, Phone, and "What do you need help with?", around 25-30% would submit. That's three times more enquiries from the same number of visitors.

The design looks dated

Your site uses a bright teal and cyan colour scheme that was popular around 2012-2016. The hero image has busy corrugated iron patterns behind the text, which makes it harder to read your main message.

What this costs you: Visitors make a trust decision within 8 seconds. If your website looks old, they assume your business might be outdated too, even though you've been successfully operating for 25 years. Modern roofing sites use deep charcoal or navy with warm accents like terracotta or coral. An updated look would stop 30-40% of visitors from bouncing back to Google.

You're missing required trust information

Queensland roofing work is regulated by the QBCC, and homeowners know to check for licence numbers before they hire anyone. Your QBCC licence number and ABN aren't displayed on your website.

What this costs you: Homeowners have been warned about unlicensed operators. When they can't find your QBCC number, many won't take the risk. They'll simply move on to a competitor who displays their credentials clearly. This is an easy fix that immediately builds trust.

Google and AI search can't find you properly

Your website is missing what's called "structured data" — a way of telling Google, ChatGPT, and other search tools exactly what services you offer, where you operate, and how to contact you.

What this costs you: When someone asks Google or ChatGPT "Who's a good roofing company in Brisbane?", the AI only recommends businesses with complete structured data. You're essentially invisible to this new generation of search. As more people use AI to find local services, you'll fall further behind competitors who've set this up.
