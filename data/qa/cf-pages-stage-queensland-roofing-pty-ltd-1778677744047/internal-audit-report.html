CRITICAL cartoon_logo_hurts_trust
Cartoon mascot logo undermines professional credibility

观察到The primary logo in the top-left shows a cartoon character with a yellow hard hat holding a trowel, paired with blue outlined text reading 'Gutter And Roof Repairs!' with '.com.au' in red below

为什么是问题When someone searches for a roofing contractor to fix a $15,000 roof problem, they want a licensed professional — not a children's cartoon character. The playful mascot makes the business look like a handyman hobby rather than a registered company, especially for high-stakes roof repairs where insurance and warranties matter.

正确长啥样A wordmark or simple icon logo using the business name 'Queensland Roofing Pty Ltd' in professional sans-serif type, possibly with a subtle roof silhouette or geometric icon, in navy or charcoal with one accent color

redesign 怎么改Replace cartoon mascot with clean wordmark logo featuring 'Queensland Roofing' in professional typography, removing all cartoon illustrations and limiting color palette to two colors maximum

MAJOR orange_overload_visual_chaos
Excessive orange elements create visual confusion

观察到The site uses bright orange (#FF8C00 range) in at least 7 distinct locations: top bar background, service icons strip at bottom of hero, form heading 'Request a Quick Quote', the 'Submit' button, multiple navigation elements, and decorative accents throughout

为什么是问题When everything is highlighted, nothing stands out. A visitor looking to call or request a quote cannot quickly identify the primary action because orange competes with orange — the eye has no clear focal point. This adds cognitive load and many visitors will leave rather than decode which orange element matters most.

正确长啥样Reserve one accent color (e.g., coral or blue) exclusively for primary conversion actions (phone button, quote form submit), use neutral grays and whites for navigation and backgrounds, limit accent color usage to 2-3 instances maximum per screen

redesign 怎么改Establish single-color CTA hierarchy: blue primary buttons for phone/quote only, remove orange top bar, convert service icons to grayscale or subtle blue tint, strip all decorative orange accents from navigation

Orange top bar pushes primary content below the fold

观察到The desktop layout has a solid orange horizontal bar spanning the full width at the very top, containing only a phone number on the left and 'Book Now' button on the right, adding approximately 45-50 pixels of vertical space before the main logo and navigation begin

为什么是问题This orange bar pushes the hero headline, value proposition, and conversion elements down the screen, wasting prime above-the-fold real estate on redundant information (the phone number appears again in the header). First-time visitors see less of the actual service offer before scrolling.

正确长啥样Remove the top bar entirely and integrate phone number as a clickable button within the main header navigation, maximizing vertical space for hero content including headline, subheadline, and primary CTA to be visible without scrolling

MAJOR navigation_menu_overload
Horizontal navigation contains too many competing items

观察到The desktop header navigation bar contains 7 top-level items: 'Gutter Services', 'Roofing Services', 'Roofing Subs' (with dropdown arrow), 'About', 'Blog', 'Contact Us', plus the 'Book Now' button, all in white text on transparent background over the hero image

为什么是问题Seven navigation choices overwhelm a first-time visitor who just searched 'roofing Brisbane' and needs a quote quickly. Research shows that every additional navigation option reduces conversion by diluting focus. Most visitors will never click 'Blog' or explore dropdown menus — they want phone or quote form immediately.

正确长啥样Desktop navigation with 3-4 essential items maximum: 'Services' (one unified dropdown), 'About', 'Contact', plus prominent phone number and 'Get Quote' CTA button visually separated from text links

redesign 怎么改Consolidate 'Gutter Services', 'Roofing Services', and 'Roofing Subs' into single 'Services' dropdown, remove 'Blog' from primary nav, move to footer, reduce nav items to 4 maximum, make phone and quote CTA visually dominant with button treatment
