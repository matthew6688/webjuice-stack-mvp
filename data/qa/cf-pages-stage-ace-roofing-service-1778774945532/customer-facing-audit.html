What this means: Your website is doing some things well, but there are clear opportunities to get more calls and quote requests. The good news? Most of these fixes are straightforward.

We've reviewed your website from the perspective of a Brisbane homeowner searching for roofing help. Here's what we found.

What's Working Well

Strong reputation on Google
Your 5-star rating across 37 reviews is excellent. That's a genuine trust signal that gives potential customers confidence when they're deciding who to call.
Clear trust credentials
You're showing the things that matter to Brisbane homeowners: licensed, insured, family-owned since 2004, and available for emergencies. These are exactly the signals people look for.
Professional roofing imagery
You're using real roofing photos rather than generic stock images, which helps visitors understand immediately that you're a legitimate local business.

What's Holding You Back

Losing calls right now
Your phone number appears cut off in the header
The blue phone button shows "0426 366 7.." with the end visually truncated. When someone's dealing with a leaking roof or storm damage, seeing an incomplete phone number creates doubt. Some visitors will simply move on to the next roofing company rather than scroll to find the full number.
Mobile visitors can't call easily
Phone number isn't click-to-call on mobile
About 60–70% of people searching for local roofing services prefer to call rather than fill out a form. On mobile, your phone number isn't tappable—visitors have to manually copy it, switch to their phone app, and paste it in. Every extra step loses customers. This is one of the easiest, highest-impact fixes.
First impression takes too long
The opening message is too long and detailed
Your hero section has a three-line headline followed by nine checklist points. Most visitors decide within seconds whether to stay or go back to Google. When there's too much text to read through, they often leave before getting to "Get Free Quote" or your phone number.
Invisible to AI search
Google and AI tools can't properly index your site
Your website is missing the structured data that Google uses to show star ratings, business hours, and service details in search results. It also means ChatGPT and other AI search tools can't accurately recommend you when people ask "Who's a reliable roofer in Brisbane?" You're essentially invisible in the new generation of search.
Trust signal
Site hasn't been updated in over a year
Your website was last updated 578 days ago. While your business is clearly active (your Google reviews are recent), the website gives the impression of a dormant business. Google and potential customers both notice this.

What Changes When We Fix This

More phone calls from mobile visitors
When your phone number is tappable and fully visible, mobile visitors—who make up the majority of "roofing near me" searches—can call you in one tap. That alone typically lifts call volume by 15–25%.
Better Google rankings and visibility
Proper structured data means your business details, ratings, and services show up in Google's knowledge panels, map results, and AI-generated summaries. You'll appear in more searches, and when you do appear, you'll stand out more.
Faster first impression that converts
A streamlined opening with a clear headline, three or four key trust points, and prominent "Get Free Quote" and "Call Now" buttons means visitors know exactly what to do within seconds. Less reading, more action.
Professional appearance that matches your reputation
Your 5-star Google rating shows you deliver excellent work. Your website should reflect that same level of care. When the site looks current and polished, it reinforces the trust your reviews have already built.
