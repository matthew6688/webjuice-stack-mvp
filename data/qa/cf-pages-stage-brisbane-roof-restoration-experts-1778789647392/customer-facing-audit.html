What's Holding You Back

We've looked at your website through the eyes of a typical Brisbane homeowner searching for roof restoration. Here's what's stopping more of them from picking up the phone.

Your main photo is too dark to see

There's a dark overlay covering your hero image, making the house photo nearly black and your headline hard to read. To a visitor making a split-second decision, it looks like the page didn't load properly.

What this costs you: Seven out of ten visitors will leave within 8 seconds if something looks broken. They'll click back to Google and try the next roofing company instead.

Your quote form asks too many questions up front

Before someone's even seen your work or credentials, your form is asking them to tick boxes about services, scope of work, work type, and roof age — more than 20 choices in total.

What this costs you: Research shows that every extra form field reduces completion by about 11%. With this many questions, you're likely losing half your potential enquiries before they finish filling it out.

There's no proof you're trustworthy above the fold

When someone lands on your site, they can't see any Google reviews, your QBCC licence number, how long you've been in business, or any insurance badges without scrolling down. For a big purchase like roof work, people need to see trust signals immediately.

What this costs you: 88% of consumers check reviews before contacting a local tradie. Without visible proof of your credibility, potential customers are clicking back to Google to find a company that shows their credentials and reviews straight away.

You have no Google reviews showing

Your business has a 4.9-star rating, but zero reviews are showing up. Google's algorithm uses review count — not just your star rating — to decide who ranks higher in local search.

What this costs you: You're likely ranking below competitors with more reviews, even if their quality is lower than yours. Every month without reviews is another month of invisible lost leads.

Your site doesn't show up in modern AI search

Tools like ChatGPT and Google's AI overviews now handle billions of searches each month. When someone asks "Who's a reliable roofer in Brisbane?", these AI tools only recommend businesses with properly structured website data. Your site is missing most of this behind-the-scenes structure.

What this costs you: You're invisible to an entirely new generation of search. As more people ask AI for recommendations instead of scrolling through Google results, you're not even in the conversation.
