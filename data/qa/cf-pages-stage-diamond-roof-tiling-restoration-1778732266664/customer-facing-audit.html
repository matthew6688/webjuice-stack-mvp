What's holding you back

Your reputation is excellent, but your website isn't doing it justice. Here are the specific issues causing you to lose customers:

Your website shows as "Not Secure"

What this means: Your site uses an old web address format (http instead of https). Modern browsers display a "Not Secure" warning in the address bar — some even show a full-screen warning before people can visit your site.

How this affects you: More than 80% of visitors will close the page immediately when they see this warning. Google also ranks insecure websites lower in search results. You're losing customers before they even see your excellent reviews.

Your phone number is hidden

What this means: When someone opens your website on their phone, they have to scroll down to find your phone number.

How this affects you: 60–70% of local service customers prefer to call rather than fill out a form. If they can't see your number immediately, many will go back to Google and call the next roofing company instead. This is one of the easiest ways to get more enquiries.

The dark design makes your site hard to read

What this means: Your website uses a black background with grey text. This style was popular years ago, but it's now difficult to read — especially on mobile phones in bright sunlight.

How this affects you: People decide whether to trust a website within 8 seconds. The dark colour scheme makes you look less professional than your competitors who use clean, modern designs with real project photos. Around 70% of mobile visitors leave immediately because the site looks outdated or hard to trust.

Your site loads very slowly

What this means: Google measured your website's loading speed at 29 out of 100 on mobile phones — that means pages take nearly 50 seconds to fully load.

How this affects you: Most people will give up and go elsewhere if a page takes more than 3 seconds to load. Slow websites also rank lower in Google search results, meaning fewer people find you in the first place.

You're missing important trust signals

What this means: Your website doesn't display your QBCC licence number, ABN, or insurance details.

How this affects you: Queensland law requires roofing contractors to display their QBCC licence. Customers specifically look for this before getting quotes — if they can't find it, they'll assume you're not properly licensed and move on to a competitor who clearly displays their credentials.
