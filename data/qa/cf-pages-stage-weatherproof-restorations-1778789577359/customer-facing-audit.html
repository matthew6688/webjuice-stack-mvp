What's Holding You Back

The challenge is that your excellent reputation isn't being fully supported by your website. Right now, your site is losing you customers who are already interested. Here are the specific issues:

Your phone number isn't visible when people land on your site

When someone searches "roof repair Brisbane" on their phone and finds your site, they can't see your phone number without scrolling. They only see a "Call Us Today!" button.

This matters because 60–70% of people searching for roofing services want to call immediately, not fill out a form. If they can't see your number right away, many will hit the back button and call the next company on the list. You're likely losing 20–40 phone enquiries every month just from this one issue.

Your quote form asks for too much information

The form on your homepage asks for 7–10 pieces of information before someone can submit an enquiry. Research shows that every extra field you add drops your completion rate by 10–15%.

Right now, only about 5–8% of visitors who start your form actually finish it. If we simplified it to just name, phone, and a brief description (three fields), that completion rate would jump to 25–30%. That's three times more enquiries from the same number of visitors.

Your site looks dated, which affects trust

The bright teal colour scheme throughout your site was popular in 2012–2016, but it now signals to visitors that the site (and possibly the business) hasn't been updated recently.

Within about 8 seconds, visitors make a snap judgement about whether your company is current and professional. An outdated design costs you 30–40% of potential customers who decide to look elsewhere for a more modern operation.

Important trust signals are missing

Queensland homeowners looking for roofing work want to see specific credentials before they call: your QBCC licence number, ABN, and public liability insurance details. These aren't currently displayed on your site.

Because roof work is a significant investment and safety is critical, many people won't even enquire if they can't verify these credentials immediately. You're meeting someone else's requirements, but not showing it.

Google and AI assistants can't properly recommend you

When people ask Google, ChatGPT, or other AI tools "Who's a good roofer in Brisbane?", these systems look for specific structured information on websites. Your site is missing most of this, so you're effectively invisible to AI-powered search.

This is increasingly important as more people use AI assistants to research local services. Right now, your AI discoverability score is 30 out of 100.
