What's Holding You Back

Your site shows a "Not Secure" warning
Your website doesn't have HTTPS security, so browsers display a "Not Secure" label in the address bar. Some browsers even show a full-screen warning before visitors can see your site. Research shows that over 80% of people will leave immediately when they see this warning. You're losing potential customers before they even read about your services.

Impact: Google has penalised sites without HTTPS in search rankings since 2018. This is directly reducing how many people find you through Google search.

The phone number is too hard to find
Your phone number appears as small white text in the blue header bar—easy to miss. About 70% of people searching for local services want to call directly rather than fill out a form. If they can't find your number quickly, they'll call the next company on the list.
The site looks dated and generic
The bright blue header bar and stock photography make the site look like a 10-year-old template. The hero image appears to be generic stock photography—there's no visible Queensland Roofing branding or recognisable Brisbane locations. This makes visitors wonder if you're actually a local Brisbane company or just a template site.

First impressions matter: Visitors form their opinion about your business within 8 seconds. An outdated design makes 40% of people question whether you're still operating or whether your work methods are current.

The quote form asks for too much information
Your contact form has 10 fields to fill out. Industry research shows that forms with more than 4 fields see a significant drop in completions—people start filling it out, get frustrated, and leave. You're losing leads who were ready to contact you.
Google can't showcase you properly
Your website is missing the technical markers that tell Google you're a local business (business hours, service areas, contact details in a format search engines understand). This means you're missing out on appearing in Google's special local business features—the Knowledge Panel, map cards, and AI-generated answers. These high-visibility spots go to competitors who have this information set up correctly.
Mobile visitors have a frustrating experience
Google's performance test scored your mobile site 51 out of 100. Pages take over 18 seconds to fully load on a typical phone connection. Most visitors will leave after 3 seconds. You're losing the majority of mobile traffic—and these days, more than half of all searches happen on phones.
