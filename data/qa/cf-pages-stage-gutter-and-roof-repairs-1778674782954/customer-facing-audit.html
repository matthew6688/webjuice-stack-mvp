What's Holding You Back

The main photo looks like a stock image, not a real project
When potential customers land on your site after searching for "roof repairs Brisbane", they want to see real before-and-after photos of local work. The current hero image shows what appears to be a staged family photo rather than actual roofing projects. Research shows that 70% of visitors will leave immediately if they don't see authentic project photos — they'll assume it's a generic template site and go back to find a competitor with real case studies.
Your phone number is hard to spot
The phone number appears in small text at the top of the page with low contrast. For emergency repairs — leaking roofs, storm damage — 40-50% of customers prefer to call immediately rather than fill out a form. If they can't find your number within three seconds, they'll ring the next company in the search results. You could be losing 15-25 direct phone enquiries every month just because the number isn't prominent enough.
The quote form asks for too much information
Your form requires seven fields including name, phone, email, full street address, suburb, postcode, and description. Industry data shows that each additional form field reduces completion by 10-15%. Many potential customers start filling it out, get frustrated halfway through, and abandon it to contact a competitor with a simpler form. Reducing to just three core fields — phone, suburb, and brief description — could double or triple your form submissions.
No trust signals appear above the fold
Your impressive 4.6-star rating and 150 reviews aren't displayed at the top of the page. 88% of local search users check reviews before deciding to make contact. If they don't see your rating and customer count immediately, most will go back to Google to find a company that prominently displays their credentials. Adding your rating, customer count, and qualifications (licensed, insured, years in business) at the top could increase enquiries by 40-60%.
Page loads slowly on mobile
Google's own data shows your site takes 2.6 seconds to display the main content on mobile — and that's the server response time alone. On slower connections, which many Brisbane suburbs still experience, visitors are waiting 7+ seconds. Most people will give up and go back to the search results within 3 seconds. Every second of delay reduces conversions, and right now you're losing potential customers before they even see your services.
