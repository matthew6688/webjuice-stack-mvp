What's working well

Your Google reviews are outstanding. Customers consistently praise your fast response times, quality workmanship, and reliability. That's a huge foundation to build on.

What's holding you back

The quote form asks too much upfront

Right now, someone who lands on your site has to fill in their name, phone, email, location, roof type, scope of work, work type, roof age, and a description before they can request a quote.

Why this matters: Most people visiting from Google aren't ready to commit to that much detail. The more fields you ask for, the more people give up and look elsewhere. A shorter form — name, phone, suburb, and service type — would capture more leads without losing quality.

The design feels dated

The logo style, heavy text shadows on the main image, and overall layout look like they're from several years ago. It doesn't match the professionalism of your actual work.

Why this matters: Roof restoration is a significant investment. People judge whether you're trustworthy in the first few seconds. If the website looks outdated, some visitors will click back to Google and choose a competitor who looks more current — even if your work is better.

No trust signals above the fold

When someone first lands on your site, they don't see your Google rating, warranty information, years in business, or proof that you're a Brisbane-based company. They have to hunt for it.

Why this matters: People are comparing multiple roofing companies. If they don't immediately see proof that you're legitimate and local, they'll move on to someone who makes it obvious.

Mobile visitors can't request a quote without scrolling

On a phone, the first screen shows the call button but no way to get a quote. Many people prefer to submit their details rather than call, especially during work hours or in public.

Why this matters: More than half of local searches happen on mobile. If someone can't quickly request a quote, you're losing leads to competitors whose sites make it easier.

The site is invisible to modern search tools

Your website is missing key technical elements that help search engines — and now AI tools like ChatGPT — understand and recommend your business. Things like properly structured business information, frequently asked questions, and service details.

Why this matters: More people are asking AI assistants "who should I call for roof restoration in Brisbane?" If your site isn't set up for these tools to read and recommend, you're invisible in a growing slice of how people search.
