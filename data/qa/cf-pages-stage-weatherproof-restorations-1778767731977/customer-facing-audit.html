What's holding you back

You've built a strong business with excellent reviews, but your website isn't converting visitors into calls the way it should. Here's what we found:

Your phone number is hidden
On both mobile and desktop, visitors can't see your phone number without clicking a button. Research shows that 60-70% of people searching for local roofing services prefer to call, not fill in forms. When someone's roof is leaking after a storm, they want to call right now—and if they can't see your number immediately, they'll hit the back button and call the next business.
Your quote form asks for too much
The form on your homepage requires visitors to fill in 7 fields before they can submit. Studies show that each extra field reduces completions by 10-15%. Right now, only 5-8% of people who start your form actually finish it. A simpler 3-field form would triple your enquiries.
The design looks dated
Your website uses a bright teal colour scheme that was popular around 2012-2016. When potential customers land on your site, they make trust decisions in under 8 seconds—and an outdated design can make them wonder if your services are also behind the times. This isn't about being trendy; it's about not giving people a reason to doubt you.
Missing trust credentials
Your QBCC licence number and ABN aren't displayed on the website. In Queensland, roofing work is regulated, and customers check these details before hiring. Not showing them prominently makes people hesitate—or worse, assume you don't have them.
Hard to find in AI search
When people ask ChatGPT or Google's AI "Who's a good roofer in Brisbane?", your website scores only 30 out of 100 for discoverability. AI search engines look for specific structured information that your current site doesn't provide, which means you're invisible to a growing number of potential customers who search this way.
