MAJOR heavy_text_shadow_hero
Heavy white text shadow on hero makes text feel dated

观察到The hero headline 'BRISBANE ROOFING SOLUTIONS' and subheading 'YOUR ROOF RESTORATION SPECIALISTS' use thick white text with heavy black drop shadows on both desktop and mobile

为什么是问题The thick shadow technique was common in 2010-2014 when designers needed text to read over busy photos, but modern visitors associate it with outdated WordPress themes — it reduces trust because it signals the site hasn't been updated in years, making visitors question if the business is still active or professional

正确长啥样White text on a 40-60% dark gradient overlay (top-to-bottom or radial from center) with no shadow, or a solid dark semi-transparent box behind the text with 24px padding and no shadow, ensuring 4.5:1 contrast without visual artifacts

redesign 怎么改Replace text shadow with a CSS linear-gradient overlay (rgba(0,0,0,0.5)) on the hero image container, remove all text-shadow properties, use 700-weight white sans-serif for the headline

MAJOR red_navigation_bar
Dark red navigation bar reduces readability and feels heavy

观察到Desktop shows a dark burgundy-red horizontal navigation bar with white text spanning the full width below the logo/phone header; mobile shows a matching red hamburger menu bar

为什么是问题The saturated dark red (#8B0000 range) creates visual weight that draws attention away from the phone number and call-to-action; white text on dark red can strain readability (borderline WCAG contrast), and the color scheme feels institutional rather than approachable for a local service business where trust and warmth matter

正确长啥样A white or light gray navigation bar with dark gray text (AA-compliant contrast), or eliminate the horizontal nav entirely on desktop and use a sticky header with logo + phone + hamburger menu that collapses to essentials, letting the hero breathe

redesign 怎么改Change navigation background to white (#FFFFFF), text to dark gray (#2C3E50), add a 1px bottom border in light gray (#E5E5E5); on mobile keep the hamburger icon but use a white background with gray icon so the phone number remains the visual priority

MAJOR desktop_cta_buttons_small
Desktop CTA buttons are small and visually compete with each other

观察到Below the hero headline on desktop are two red buttons side-by-side ('REQUEST A QUOTE' and 'EMERGENCY ROOFING'), both similar size (approximately 140-160px wide, 36-40px tall) with identical red background and white text

为什么是问题When two CTAs are identical in style and size, visitors experience decision paralysis — neither stands out as the primary action, reducing clicks on both; the buttons are also small relative to the large hero area, so desktop users may scroll past them looking for a more prominent contact method

正确长啥样Single primary CTA button minimum 200px wide × 56px tall on desktop with high-contrast color (e.g. coral #FF6B6B or safety-orange #FF8C42), secondary action styled as ghost button (white border, transparent fill) or text link below; positioned centrally with 40px vertical spacing

redesign 怎么改Make 'REQUEST A QUOTE' the primary button: 240px × 64px, coral background, bold white text, 4px border-radius; move 'EMERGENCY ROOFING' to a secondary ghost-style button below (white border, no fill, 200px × 48px), or integrate emergency phone as a small badge in the header instead

MINOR mobile_hamburger_above_hero
Mobile hamburger menu takes valuable above-fold space

观察到Mobile screenshot shows a full-width red bar with centered hamburger icon (≡) sitting between the logo/phone area and the hero image, occupying approximately 48-52px of vertical space

为什么是问题On mobile, every pixel above the fold is precious — the hamburger bar pushes the hero headline and CTA further down, and most local-search mobile visitors (85%+) want to call immediately, not explore menu pages; the hamburger also isn't universally understood by older demographics in trades services

正确长啥样Logo + click-to-call phone number in a 60px sticky header, hamburger icon (if needed) integrated as a small icon in top-right corner of that header (not a separate bar), hero image starts immediately below at ~60px from top with headline overlaying the image

redesign 怎么改Consolidate header into single 60px bar: left-align logo (120px wide), right-align phone number as tappable link (green phone icon + number), place hamburger as 20×20px icon in far top-right; remove the separate red menu bar entirely, reclaim 48px vertical space for hero content

MINOR hero_photo_generic_angle
Hero photo shows generic suburban roof, not the work itself

观察到Both desktop and mobile hero images show a wide-angle photo of a brick house with a tiled roof from street level, standard residential photography with the house centered in frame

为什么是问题Local service customers searching for roof restoration want to see proof of quality workmanship — before/after comparisons, close-ups of restored tiles, team at work — not a generic house photo that could be stock imagery; this misses an opportunity to differentiate from competitors and build immediate visual trust

正确长啥样Hero image showing a close-up of freshly restored terracotta roof tiles (texture visible, professional result clear), or a split before/after image with a vertical divider, or a photo of uniformed crew working on a Brisbane roof with safety equipment visible — something that proves capability

redesign 怎么改Replace hero with client-supplied before/after slider (if available) or a tight shot of completed roof restoration work; if no professional photos exist, use a photo of the actual crew with branded van/uniform as hero, then add before/after gallery immediately below the fold
