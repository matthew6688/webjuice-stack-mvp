What's Holding You Back

When people land on your website, they make a decision in about 8 seconds: call you, or try the next company. Right now, several things are working against you in those critical first moments.

Outdated Design Reduces Trust

Your website uses design patterns from 2012—heavy shadows, dark red navigation bars, and dated visual effects. Research shows that 30–40% of visitors will assume a business with an outdated website might not be actively trading, and they'll leave to find a competitor with a more modern presence.

You're Invisible to New Search Technology

Your website is missing the structured information that Google and AI search tools (like ChatGPT) need to recommend local businesses. When someone asks "Who's a reliable roofing company in Brisbane?", you're not showing up in those answers—even though you have 119 five-star reviews. This is like having an unlisted phone number when everyone's using directory assistance.

Google Can't Tell What You Do

The browser tab title for your website doesn't clearly state your services or location. This is one of the biggest factors Google uses to decide what searches to show your site for. It's a quick fix, but it's costing you rankings right now.

Your Contact Form is Too Long

You're asking visitors to fill in 9 fields before they can request a quote. Industry data shows that every extra field reduces the number of people who complete the form. The standard for local services is 3–4 fields. You're likely losing 15–25% of potential enquiries just from form friction.

Competing Buttons Create Confusion

Your homepage shows two identical red buttons side-by-side—"Request a Quote" and "Emergency Roofing"—and they look exactly the same. When visitors can't tell which action to take, many choose neither. Clear visual hierarchy drives action; identical buttons cause hesitation.

Mobile Page Speed is Slow

Google rates your mobile site performance at 54 out of 100. Most of your visitors are on phones with patchy reception. A slow site means people give up before the page even loads, especially if they're comparing multiple roofers at once.
