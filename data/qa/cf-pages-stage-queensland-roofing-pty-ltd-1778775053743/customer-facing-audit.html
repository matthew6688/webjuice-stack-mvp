What's Holding You Back

Your website shows "Not Secure" in the browser

What this means: Your site doesn't have HTTPS security. When someone visits, their browser displays a "Not Secure" warning in the address bar. Some browsers even show a full-screen warning that blocks the page.

Impact on your business: Google has penalised websites without HTTPS since 2018. More than 80% of visitors will close the tab the moment they see "Not Secure" — especially when they're about to share their phone number and home address. For a business with zero Google reviews, this warning alone is costing you dozens of enquiries every month.

The design looks a decade out of date

What this means: The gradient navigation bar, embossed logo, and split homepage layout are visual styles from around 2012. To a visitor in 2026, the site signals that it hasn't been touched in ten years.

Impact on your business: Visitors judge a website's trustworthiness in about eight seconds. An outdated appearance causes roughly 40% of first-time visitors to close the page immediately and click the next result in Google — even if your service is better and your prices are more competitive. You're losing jobs to competitors with worse skills but newer websites.

Your enquiry form asks for too much too soon

What this means: Your contact form asks for name, phone, full street address, email, service type, and a description box — at least six fields — before someone has even read a single word about your business.

Impact on your business: Industry research shows that every extra form field reduces completion rates by about 11%. A six-field form can generate 50% fewer enquiries than a two-field form. That could mean dozens of lost leads every month — people who wanted a quote but gave up halfway through filling out the form.

No Google reviews or star rating visible

What this means: Your homepage doesn't display your Google star rating, review count, or any customer testimonials above the fold. The only trust signal is the small "since 1975" line in your logo.

Impact on your business: More than 90% of consumers check online reviews before booking a local tradie. Displaying your Google rating prominently on the homepage can increase enquiries by 20–30%, because visitors don't need to leave your site to verify your reputation — which means fewer of them click away to check out your competitors instead.

No clear headline to confirm what you do

What this means: Your homepage hero area is split 50/50 between a worker photo and a house photo, but there's no large headline text anywhere. A visitor landing on the page can't immediately see a message like "Brisbane's Trusted Roofer Since 1975."

Impact on your business: Visitors spend an average of 15 seconds on a page. Without a clear headline, many will leave because they can't quickly confirm you solve their problem. Adding a prominent first-screen headline typically increases visitor engagement by about 30%, which translates directly into more quote requests.

Your phone number doesn't look like a button

What this means: The phone number in your header appears as plain styled text, without a coloured background, border, or visual cue that it's clickable. It blends in with the navigation links.

Impact on your business: For local service websites, the phone is the most important conversion path. Without a clear button style, 30–40% of visitors may not notice or click the number. That's potentially dozens of missed phone calls every month — calls that would have turned into booked jobs.

What Changes When We Fix This

More trust, more enquiries: A secure (HTTPS) site with a modern, clean design immediately signals that you're a professional, active business. The "Not Secure" warning disappears, and visitors stay on the page instead of bouncing to a competitor.

Double the form completions: Reducing your enquiry form from six fields to three (name, mobile, service type) can dramatically increase completion rates. Fewer fields mean less friction — and more leads in your inbox.

Visitors know what you do in three seconds: A full-width hero image with a clear headline ("Brisbane's Trusted Roofer Since 1975 — Repairs, Restoration & Replacement") and a high-contrast "Call for Free Quote" button means visitors immediately understand what you offer and how to contact you.

Your Google rating becomes a selling point: Displaying your star rating and review count in the header — and featuring a customer testimonial below the hero — gives visitors instant social proof. They see that other Brisbane homeowners trust you, which makes them far more likely to pick up the phone.

More calls from mobile visitors: Turning your phone number into a bright, prominent button makes it the most visible element on the page. On mobile, it becomes a single-tap action. That means more calls from people who are ready to book.

Faster loading, better Google ranking: Right now, your mobile site takes nearly 13 seconds to load the main content. Optimising images and cleaning up unnecessary scripts can cut that to under three seconds — which not only keeps visitors on the page but also improves your position in Google search results.
