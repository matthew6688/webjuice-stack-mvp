What's Holding You Back

Your phone number is hard to find
Most people searching for roof repairs want to call, not fill out a form. Your phone number is tucked away in small text at the top of the page—easy to miss, especially on a mobile. When people can't find your number quickly, they click back and call the next company on Google.
Your quote form asks for too much information
Right now visitors see eight separate fields to fill in, plus checkboxes and a text area with a typo. Research shows that every extra field you ask for reduces the number of people who'll complete it. Most folks just want to give you their name, number, and a quick description—they'll happily provide the rest when you call them back.
Too many buttons compete for attention
Your homepage shows three different ways to contact you: a "Request a Quote" button in the header, a "Contact Us" button in the hero section, and the quote form on the right. When visitors see multiple options, they often freeze and pick none. One clear path—like a prominent phone number—gets better results.
Search engines can't read your business details
When someone searches for "roof restoration near me," Google (and increasingly, ChatGPT and other AI tools) look for structured information about local businesses—your address, opening hours, services, and reviews. Your site doesn't include this in the format they expect, which means you're missing out on appearing in map results, knowledge panels, and AI-generated recommendations.
Your navigation is crowded
Seven items across the top of your site make it harder for visitors to quickly find what they need. Simpler navigation—three or four clear options plus a standout phone number—helps people take action faster.
